Signature Event Comparison — Unity BBQ vs Neighborhood Block Party

Printed from the NPHC of Hudson County event comparison web report.

Executive Summary

The committee evaluated two proposed signature event concepts across nine strategic criteria: community impact, NPHC alignment, feasibility, partnerships, member engagement, unity, signature potential, capacity requirements, and strategic fit.
Unity BBQ: internal fellowship + inter-organizational relationship-building Neighborhood Block Party: community-facing back-to-school initiative

Key Context (from chapter assessment & findings)

  • Documented “ticket fatigue” and member preference for more accessible programming.
  • Leadership capacity constraints (vacancies and reliance on a small core group).
  • Hudson County scale and density create significant community engagement upside.
  • Current civic engagement footprint noted as minimal relative to county population.

Concept 1 — Divine Nine Unity BBQ

A fellowship-centered gathering to build trust, strengthen working relationships across organizations, support reclamation/re-engagement, and establish foundations for future joint programming.
Strength areas include feasibility (lower complexity) and inter-organizational unity.

Concept 2 — Neighborhood Block Party (Back-to-School)

A community-facing event providing school supplies/backpacks, family engagement, and high-visibility public relationship-building with partners, sponsors, and civic stakeholders.
Strength areas include community impact, NPHC pillar alignment, partnership potential, and signature-event brand building.

Budget & Financial Considerations

Unity BBQ (Estimated)

  • Total expenses: $2,100–$4,100
  • Typical cost drivers: venue, food, entertainment, supplies
  • Lower sponsorship appeal due to internal audience

Block Party (Estimated)

  • Total expenses: $5,000–$10,000
  • Includes permits/insurance, supplies/backpacks, entertainment, food, signage
  • Higher sponsorship and in-kind donation potential

Risk Snapshot

Block Party — Risk Areas

Weather, permits, volunteer capacity, supply availability, safety planning, vendor coordination. Overall profile described as moderate and manageable with strong coordination.

Unity BBQ — Risk Areas

Attendance variability, weather (if outdoors), food execution, and ensuring cross-organization interaction. Overall profile described as low with straightforward execution.

Success Metrics

Block Party (Year 1 Targets)

  • Attendance: 200–300
  • Backpacks distributed: 100
  • Volunteers: 25–30
  • Sponsorship secured: $2,000–$4,000
  • Partners engaged: 10–15

Unity BBQ (Operational Indicators)

  • Total attendance by organization
  • Representation across Divine Nine chapters
  • Member feedback & engagement
  • Inter-organizational relationship quality
  • Post-event collaboration opportunities identified
